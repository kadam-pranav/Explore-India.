The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, is one of the most iconic monuments in the world and a symbol of eternal love. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died in 1631 during childbirth. Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was completed in 1653. This magnificent structure is made of white marble and is a fine example of Mughal architecture, which combines elements of Persian, Islamic, and Indian styles. The Taj Mahal stands on the banks of the Yamuna River and features a large central dome, four minarets, a beautiful garden, and intricate carvings. The monument changes color depending on the time of day — appearing pinkish in the morning, milky white in the evening, and golden under moonlight. In 1983, it was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and today, it is one of the Seven Wonders of the World. The Taj Mahal not only reflects India’s rich history and artistry but also stands as a timeless symbol of love and devotion. Every year, millions of tourists from around the globe visit this architectural masterpiece.

About India






Narendra Modi, the 14th Prime Minister of India, is known for his dynamic leadership and transformative vision. Born on 17th September 1950 in Vadnagar, Gujarat, Modi rose from humble beginnings, once selling tea at a railway station, to become one of the most influential leaders in Indian history. He served as the Chief Minister of Gujarat from 2001 to 2014, where he focused on development, infrastructure, and industrial growth. As Prime Minister since 2014, he launched several ambitious initiatives like Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Clean India Mission), Digital India, Make in India, and Startup India, aiming to boost the economy, cleanliness, innovation, and employment. His foreign policy has strengthened India’s global presence. Modi is admired for his strong communication skills, nationalism, and focus on good governance. His leadership during the COVID-19 pandemic and the push for vaccination were globally recognized. However, his tenure has also faced criticism over issues like press freedom and communal tensions. Despite controversies, Narendra Modi continues to shape India’s future with a blend of tradition and modernity, aiming to make India a global power in the 21st century. His journey inspires millions across the nation.

Nature has made India one of the luckiest countries in the world.
